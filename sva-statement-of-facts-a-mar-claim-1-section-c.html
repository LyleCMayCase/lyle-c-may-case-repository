This Source Verification Page documents a court filing submitted on behalf of Lyle Clinton May during his post-conviction proceedings arising from the July 1997 murders in Asheville, North Carolina. The Motion for Appropriate Relief is referenced within the Supplemental Motion to demonstrate that specific claims concerning the reliability of the confession and supporting evidence were previously presented to the state courts. This page preserves the citation and identifies the filing relied upon in Statement of Facts A.

Cited to Support

Lyle C. May alleges that his unrecorded confession was unreliable and should not have been admitted because his severe intoxication, serious mental illness, youth, and the circumstances of the interrogation prevented a knowing waiver of rights and rendered any confession unreliable. May cites MAR Claim 1, Section C to establish that these allegations were previously raised in his original Motion for Appropriate Relief and supported by mental health expert affidavits. He further relies upon this claim to argue that if the confession is determined to be inadmissible or false, the physical evidence in the case becomes substantially more important in evaluating guilt or innocence.
