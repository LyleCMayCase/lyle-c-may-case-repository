Cited to Support

According to the Supplemental Motion, Officer Judy Hensley did not testify at the Defendant's 1999 suppression hearing or trial in Asheville, North Carolina, despite allegedly witnessing events central to the interrogation. The motion further argues that prosecutors instead relied upon Detective Judy Romick's testimony concerning matters observed by Hensley, preventing direct cross-examination of a witness whom Lyle C. May contends possessed critical information regarding the circumstances under which his statement was obtained.

Officer Judy Hensley observed May showing signs of fatigue and repeatedly attempting to rest while remaining in police custody for several hours before interrogation as detectives Judy Romick and Mike Downing interviewed Darrell Godfrey. Hensley's statement is cited to establish that May appeared tired, attempted to sleep, and was kept awake by law enforcement personnel during the period preceding his interrogation. May relies upon these observations to support his claim regarding his physical and mental condition prior to questioning and the circumstances and conditions surrounding the obtaining of his subsequent statements.
