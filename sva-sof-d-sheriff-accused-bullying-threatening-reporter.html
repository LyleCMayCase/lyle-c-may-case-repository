Sheriff Accused of Bullying, Threatening Reporter

SUPPLEMENTAL ARTICLE SOURCE ENTRY

Last Revised • July 7, 2026

This Source Verification Page documents a supplemental news article published by the Reporters Committee for Freedom of the Press concerning allegations that Buncombe County Sheriff Bobby Medford threatened Asheville Citizen-Times reporter Tonya Maxwell following her investigative reporting on the Mary Judd homicide investigation. The article provides independent corroboration of the reporting referenced in Statement of Facts D and supplies additional context regarding official reactions to media coverage of the investigation.

Cited to Support

May's allegation that public reporting by the Asheville Citizen-Times exposed significant problems in the investigation of the Mary Judd homicide, prompting District Attorney Ron Moore to review the case and defend the actions of the Buncombe County Sheriff's Office. The Reporters Committee article is cited to corroborate that Sheriff Bobby Medford reacted to the newspaper's reporting by threatening reporter Tonya Maxwell, that the Citizen-Times' investigative series "Waiting for Justice" prompted Moore's review of the investigation, and that Moore's subsequent report concluded there was no wrongdoing by the Sheriff's Department despite the controversy surrounding the investigation.
