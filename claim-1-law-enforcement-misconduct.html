Claim I

LAW ENFORCEMENT MISCONDUCT

Last Revised • June 30, 2026

Official Claim Title

LAW ENFORCEMENT MISCONDUCT CAUSED THE DEFENDANT TO BE DENIED A FAIR TRIAL, DUE PROCESS, AND HIS RIGHT TO BE PROTECTED FROM WITNESSING AGAINST HIMSELF, PROTECTED BY THE FIFTH, SIXTH, AND FOURTEENTH AMENDMENTS OF THE UNITED STATES CONSTITUTION AND ARTICLE I, SECTIONS 19, 23, 24, 27, AND 36 OF THE NORTH CAROLINA CONSTITUTION.

Claim Overview

Claim I alleges that law enforcement misconduct during the investigation and collection of evidence deprived the defendant of constitutional protections guaranteed under the United States and North Carolina Constitutions. This section of the Supplemental Motion addresses custodial interrogation practices, Miranda issues, and allegations relating to the handling and preservation of evidence.

Issues Presented

Miranda Violation

Discussion of custodial interrogation, Miranda warnings, and the admissibility of statements obtained during questioning.
