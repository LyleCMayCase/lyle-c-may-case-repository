Audit Reveals Missing Guns, Drugs and Money at Buncombe Sheriff's Department

ARTICLE SOURCE ENTRY

Last Revised • July 6, 2026

This Source Verification Page documents a newspaper article reporting the results of a 2006 audit of the Buncombe County Sheriff's Department evidence storage and inventory system. The article describes significant deficiencies involving missing evidence, firearms, drugs, and currency, providing independent reporting relevant to the preservation and reliability of physical evidence discussed throughout Statement of Facts B.

Cited to Support

May's allegation that serious deficiencies existed within the Buncombe County Sheriff's Department evidence storage and inventory system. The Mountain Xpress article is cited to document the results of a 2006 audit conducted during the transition between sheriffs, which reportedly found that a substantial percentage of evidence items were either missing, could not be located, or had been compromised. May relies upon this source to support his claim that the integrity of evidence maintained by Buncombe County law enforcement was subject to significant deficiencies, raising concerns regarding the preservation, accountability, and reliability of physical evidence relevant to criminal investigations and prosecutions.
