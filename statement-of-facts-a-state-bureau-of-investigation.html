Statement of Facts: A

STATE BUREAU OF INVESTIGATION

Last Revised • July 2, 2026

Statement of Facts A addresses issues relating to the North Carolina State Bureau of Investigation (SBI) Crime Laboratory and the forensic evidence presented in the prosecution of Lyle C. May. The Supplemental Motion for Appropriate Relief argues that subsequent investigations, court findings, and independent reviews revealed significant concerns regarding SBI laboratory practices during the period relevant to Mr. May's case. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

The motion relies heavily on the findings of the 2010 Independent Review of the SBI Forensic Laboratory, commonly known as the Swecker Report, which examined forensic reporting practices within the SBI Crime Laboratory and identified cases involving omitted, incomplete, or misleading laboratory reporting. The motion further cites later court rulings, public statements, and additional reviews that raised questions about the reliability of certain forensic analyses and the disclosure of evidence favorable to criminal defendants. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

The section then examines the SBI's involvement in Mr. May's prosecution, including the forensic testing presented at trial, the testimony of SBI analysts, and the role that DNA and physical evidence played in the State's case. According to the motion, these issues take on increased significance because Mr. May's original Motion for Appropriate Relief challenges the admissibility and reliability of his alleged confession, potentially elevating the importance of the physical evidence presented to the jury. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

The sources collected within this section allow readers to review the reports, court orders, forensic records, and historical materials referenced in support of these claims.
