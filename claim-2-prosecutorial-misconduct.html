Claim II

PROSECUTORIAL MISCONDUCT

Last Revised • June 30, 2026

Official Claim Title

CLAIM II. PROSECUTORIAL MISCONDUCT CAUSED THE DEFENDANT TO BE DENIED A FAIR TRIAL, THE RIGHT TO CONFRONT THE WITNESSES AGAINST HIM, AND DUE PROCESS, PROTECTED BY THE FIFTH, SIXTH, AND FOURTEENTH AMENDMENTS TO THE UNITED STATES CONSTITUTION AND ARTICLE I, SECTIONS 19, 23, 24, 27, AND 36 OF THE NORTH CAROLINA CONSTITUTION.

Claim Overview

Claim II alleges that prosecutorial misconduct during the investigation and prosecution deprived the defendant of constitutional protections guaranteed under the United States and North Carolina Constitutions. This claim addresses allegations relating to disclosure obligations, trial conduct, and due process.

Issues Presented

Suppression of Exculpatory Evidence

Allegations that prosecutors withheld material evidence favorable to the defense, including witness testimony and investigative records.
