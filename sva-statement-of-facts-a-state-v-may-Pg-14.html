State v. May

354 N.C. 172, 552 S.E.2d 151 (2001)

CASE ENTRY

Last Revised • July 4, 2026

State v. May is the opinion issued by the North Carolina Supreme Court on direct appeal from the convictions and death sentences of Lyle C. May arising from the July 1997 murders in Asheville, North Carolina. The Court reviewed the issues raised on appeal and ultimately found no reversible error, affirming both the convictions and the sentences imposed by the trial court. This opinion is cited throughout the Supplemental Motion to document the procedural history of the case and the North Carolina Supreme Court's discussion of the forensic evidence presented at trial.

Cited to Support

Lyle C. May alleges that the North Carolina Supreme Court, in affirming his convictions and death sentences, characterized the forensic evidence presented at trial as significant evidence indicating guilt. May cites State v. May to identify the Court's reliance upon DNA evidence and other forensic evidence in affirming his convictions. May relies upon this portion of the opinion to support his claim that the Court's assessment was based upon forensic evidence and testimony that he alleges were misleadingly presented and insufficient, standing alone, to substantiate guilt.
