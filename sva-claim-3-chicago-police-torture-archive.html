Chicago Police Torture Archive

WEBSITE ENTRY

Last Revised • July 11, 2026

This Source Verification Page documents the official website of the Chicago Police Torture Archive, cited in Claim III of the Supplemental Motion. The archive is relied upon as a publicly available historical resource documenting investigations, litigation, and records relating to allegations of systemic police torture and official misconduct associated with former Chicago Police Commander Jon Burge. It is included to provide supporting background information for the allegations discussed in Paragraph 128 of the Supplemental Motion.

Cited to Support

May's allegation that Chicago police detectives operating under Commander Jon Burge engaged in a longstanding pattern of physical and psychological coercion during custodial interrogations; that the resulting misconduct contributed to wrongful convictions, death sentences, post-conviction litigation, exonerations, pardons, and official investigations; and that the full scope of the misconduct was exposed through independent review, investigative efforts, civil litigation, and subsequent state and federal proceedings. May cites the Chicago Police Torture Archive as supporting background information regarding the Burge torture cases and to support his argument that independent investigation may be necessary to uncover and develop evidence of widespread systemic misconduct.
