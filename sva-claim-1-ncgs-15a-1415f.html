N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-1415(f)

NORTH CAROLINA GENERAL STATUTE ENTRY

Last Revised • July 9, 2026

This Source Verification Page documents North Carolina General Statute § 15A-1415(f), cited in Claim I of the Supplemental Motion. This statute establishes discovery rights available to post-conviction counsel in capital cases and is cited to support the Defendant's claim regarding access to investigative, prosecutorial, and law-enforcement records relevant to his post-conviction proceedings.

Statutory Purpose

North Carolina General Statute § 15A-1415(f) governs post-conviction discovery in capital cases and specifies the categories of investigative and prosecutorial records that may be made available to post-conviction counsel, subject to the limitations established by law.

Cited to Support

May's reliance upon N.C. Gen. Stat. § 15A-1415(f) for the statutory requirement that postconviction counsel in capital cases be provided access to the complete files of all law enforcement and prosecutorial agencies involved in the investigation and prosecution of the defendant, subject only to limited statutory exceptions. May cites this statute in support of his claim that he was entitled to discovery of investigative and prosecutorial files, including materials relating to the handling, preservation, and testing of physical evidence in his case.
