Brady v. Maryland

373 U.S. 83 (1963)

Last Revised • July 2, 2026

Overview

Brady v. Maryland is a landmark United States Supreme Court decision establishing that the prosecution violates due process when it suppresses material evidence favorable to the accused. The decision forms the constitutional foundation for the prosecution's obligation to disclose exculpatory and impeachment evidence to the defense.

Why This Source Was Cited

The Supplemental Motion cites Brady v. Maryland as the foundational authority establishing the prosecution's constitutional duty to disclose favorable evidence to the defense. The case provides the legal framework supporting the allegations that material evidence was withheld in violation of the Defendant's due process rights.
