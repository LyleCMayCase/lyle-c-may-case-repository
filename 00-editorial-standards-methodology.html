Editorial Standards

The Lyle C. May Case Repository organizes publicly available documents associated with the case of Lyle C. May. Materials are presented as accurately as possible while preserving the original language, formatting, and context of the underlying sources.

These standards are intended to promote transparency, consistency, and ease of verification for researchers, journalists, attorneys, students, and other members of the public.

Source Verification

Whenever possible, citations are verified against original court opinions, court records, government publications, or other primary source materials. Secondary sources are clearly identified when used, and readers are encouraged to consult original source documents whenever available.

Organization

Repository materials are organized by collection and, where appropriate, linked directly to the legal claims discussed in the Supplemental Motion. Supporting authorities are grouped within the Source Verification Archive to allow readers to locate cited materials efficiently.

Corrections

If you believe a citation, transcription, attribution, link, or document reference contains an error, please use the repository's contact page so the information can be reviewed and corrected when appropriate.
