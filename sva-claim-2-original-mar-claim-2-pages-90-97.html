State v. Lyle Clinton May Motion for Appropriate Relief

COURT FILING ENTRY

Last Revised • July 10, 2026

This Source Verification Page documents the Defendant's original Motion for Appropriate Relief, specifically Claim II, cited in the Supplemental Motion. The filing is referenced to demonstrate that allegations concerning Darrell Godfrey's credibility, criminal history, and prosecutorial misconduct were originally presented in the Defendant's post-conviction proceedings.

Cited to Support

May's allegation that prosecutors misled the jury regarding Darrell Godfrey's status and potential criminal exposure by creating the impression that Godfrey faced prosecution for the murders rather than serving as a cooperating witness charged only with accessory-after-the-fact offenses; that this issue was previously raised in Claim II of the Defendant's original Motion for Appropriate Relief; and that the original claim alleged prosecutorial misconduct deprived the Defendant of a fair trial. May further alleges that Judge Dennis Winner's denial of Claim II as harmless error occurred without consideration of the additional evidence and allegations presented in the Supplemental MAR. May cites the original MAR to support his request that the Court revisit and reconsider Claim II in light of the cumulative evidence now presented.
