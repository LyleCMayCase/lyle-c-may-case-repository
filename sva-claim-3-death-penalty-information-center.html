Death Penalty Information Center

WEBSITE ENTRY

Last Revised • July 11, 2026

This Source Verification Page documents the official website of the Death Penalty Information Center, cited in Claim III of the Supplemental Motion. The organization provides publicly available research, reports, and educational resources concerning capital punishment, wrongful convictions, post-conviction litigation, and criminal justice reform. This source is included to provide supporting background information relating to the allegations discussed in Paragraph 128 of the Supplemental Motion.

Cited to Support

May's allegation that the Burge torture cases resulted in wrongful convictions, death sentences, exonerations, pardons, and extensive post-conviction litigation after allegations of systemic misconduct by law enforcement officers were independently investigated and exposed. May cites the Death Penalty Information Center as supporting background information regarding the impact of the Burge cases and the resulting review of affected convictions and death sentences. May relies upon this source to support his argument that independent investigation may be necessary to uncover, evaluate, and remedy widespread misconduct affecting criminal convictions.
